# Capital Velocity Gap: Rust Belt Innovation Funding Efficiency Crisis

**A Multi-State Forensic Analysis of State Innovation Funding Intermediaries**

By Ajmail Matin  
Executive MBA Candidate, Joseph M. Katz Graduate School of Business, University of Pittsburgh  
April 2026

---

## Overview

State innovation funding in the Rust Belt operates as an overhead absorption machine. This forensic analysis of five states (PA, OH, MI, WI, IN) reveals that on average, **only 27 cents of every state dollar reaches founders as direct capital**. The remaining 73 cents is consumed by intermediary operations.

## Key Findings

### Direct Capital Ratios by State

| State | Direct Capital Ratio | Structure |
|-------|---------------------|-----------|
| **Pennsylvania** | **23%** (avg) | Fragmented dual-track: 4 BFTP centers + ~24 state-backed VCs |
| **Ohio** | **21%** | Regional intermediaries (JumpStart, Rev1) |
| **Michigan** | **33%** (avg) | Mixed model (Invest Detroit, Ann Arbor SPARK) |
| **Wisconsin** | **20%** | WEDC + CDE layer |
| **Indiana** | **55%** | Single consolidated vehicle (Elevate Ventures) |

### Pennsylvania's Efficiency Crisis

**Philadelphia's Ben Franklin Technology Partners (BFTP-SEP):**
- **0.2% Direct Capital Ratio**
- 99.8% of state funding absorbed by organizational overhead before reaching founders
- Strongest evidence for "dead money" thesis

**Pennsylvania's Dual-Track Problem:**
- State pays intermediary salaries at BFTP centers
- State pays management fees to ~24 venture capital firms
- No coordination between tracks
- No unified outcome accountability
- Zero customer access mandate

**Indiana's Consolidated Model:**
- Elevate Ventures: single statewide vehicle
- 55% Direct Capital Ratio (2.4x Pennsylvania's efficiency)
- Formal pilot programs linking Eli Lilly, Anthem, Cummins to funded startups

## Root Cause: The Obsolete 1983 Model

The "intermediary model" originated in 1983 when founders lacked both capital and the knowledge to deploy it. The model bundled investment with:
- Mentorship
- Business planning
- Market research
- Technical assistance

**The AI inflection point:** Recent field research (Kim, Kim, & Koning 2026) shows AI-enabled startups grow **1.9x faster** while requiring **39.5% less external capital**. The bundled services intermediaries provide are now available to founders at near-zero marginal cost.

This research paper was completed in approximately **one hour** using AI-assisted methods—demonstrating the same capabilities founders now use to navigate market discovery and strategy without high-overhead intermediaries.

## The Missing Link: Customers

**Pennsylvania's insurance premium tax credit model:**
- $100M in tax credits sold to 29 insurers at 87 cents on the dollar
- **Zero statutory requirement** for insurers to pilot startup products
- No "first customer" mandate tied to tax incentives

**Indiana's customer-access infrastructure:**
- Formal pilot programs with corporate anchors (Eli Lilly, Anthem, Cummins)
- Anthem innovation studio develops products for internal deployment
- Creates direct path from funding to revenue

**Capital without customers funds founder relocation, not ecosystem retention.**

## Three-Part Reform Proposal

### 1. 75% Direct Capital Mandate
Require at least 75% of state innovation funding be disbursed directly to founders as capital.

**Impact:** Would nearly triple capital available to Pennsylvania founders without increasing state appropriations. For every $10M allocated, $7.5M would reach founders as investment (vs. $2.7M under current structures).

### 2. First Customer Clause
Require corporations receiving state tax incentives above threshold ($10M annually) to pilot at least one product per year from state-headquartered startup.

**Impact:** Converts tax incentive programs from pure capital supply mechanisms into ecosystem retention tools.

### 3. Architectural Consolidation
Consolidate fragmented funding tracks into single high-velocity deployment vehicle, or mandate unified outcome reporting across all state-backed capital sources.

**Impact:** Eliminates duplicative overhead where states pay both intermediary salaries AND VC management fees with no coordination.

## Methodology

### The Direct Capital Ratio (DCR)

**DCR = Direct Capital to Founders ÷ Total State Funding Received**

**Numerator:** Direct grants, equity investments, and convertible notes disbursed to founding teams.

**Denominator:** Total state-designated appropriations received by the intermediary (excluding federal pass-through funds).

**Overhead:** Salaries, "technical assistance," consulting fees, rent, and administrative expenses are categorized strictly as overhead, regardless of how intermediaries characterize them in program reporting.
